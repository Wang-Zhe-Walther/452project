Global PC Market Experiences Steady Growth in Q4 2024

December 5, 2024

In the fourth quarter of 2024, the global personal computer market continued its steady growth trend. Total shipments of desktops and laptops increased by 3.6% year-over-year, reaching nearly 60 million units. The shipment volume of laptops (including mobile workstations) grew by 4.2% to about 47.5 million units, while desktop PCs saw a slight increase of 1.8%, with shipments totaling 12.5 million units. This growth is attributed to robust sales during the holiday season, as well as ongoing interest in AI-enhanced PCs and the Windows 11 upgrade cycle.

Gartner Reports Positive Trends in Global PC Market for Q2 2024

July 1, 2024

Gartner has released its analysis for the second quarter of 2024, indicating that the global personal computer market maintained its positive momentum from the previous quarter. Total shipments of desktops and laptops grew by 3.5% year-over-year, reaching approximately 58 million units. Notably, the shipment volume of laptops surged by 4.7% to about 46 million units, driven by increased commercial demand and consumer interest in newer models equipped with advanced AI capabilities. Desktop shipments also showed a slight improvement, increasing by 0.8% to around 12 million units. The continued adoption of Windows 11 and the introduction of innovative features have played a crucial role in this growth.

IDC: Third Quarter 2024 Sees Continued Expansion in PC Shipments

October 1, 2024

According to IDC's latest report, the third quarter of 2024 saw the global PC market expand further, with total shipments of desktops and laptops rising by 3.2% compared to the same period last year, totaling nearly 59 million units. Laptops (including mobile workstations) experienced a significant increase of 4.5%, reaching 47 million units. Desktop PCs (including desktop workstations) also saw a modest uptick of 0.9%, with shipments amounting to just over 11 million units. This ongoing growth is attributed to strong sales in both consumer and business segments, fueled by the availability of new AI-enhanced PCs and the widespread transition to Windows 11, which offers improved performance and security features.
